An article about how debt bondage can actually mortgage your future

Debt Mortgages your Future
by Dianne Becker

To dream the impossible dream
To fight the unbeatable foe
To bear the unbearable sorrow
To run where the brave dare not go. . .*

What are your dreams? What are your hopes? What are your aspirations? Are you able to look through today and dream about tomorrow or are anxiety and worry about your finances blocking your vision for the future?

Is your vision for tomorrow clouded by the debt of yesterday? Is there too much month left at the end of your paycheck?

If you have answered yes to any of these questions, then maybe you have mortgaged your future.

To mortgage your future means to limit your future choices. The Bible says, “The rich rule over the poor, and the borrower is servant to the lender.” (Prov. 22:17 NIV) To mortgage your future means that you set yourself up for a lifetime of servitude to your lender. For example—being in debt bondage makes it harder to buy a house, start a business or devote yourself to kingdom service. That, in turn limits what God can do through you. Debt may also lower your standard of living and cause stress in your marriage. Debt also ties up your money and also obligates you to make payments from money not yet earned.

Would you like to be free of debt bondage? Would you like to be free to make choices about your future?

Consider taking a class that may change your life and certainly will change your finances. Financial Freedom in 7 Weeks Plus will set you on track with your finances by helping you set up a true financial freedom plan to meet your personal needs and to help you leave a legacy that will make God smile.

* From Man of La Mancha, Lyrics by Joe Darion
